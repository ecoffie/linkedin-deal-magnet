# Generate LinkedIn Headline

Generate an optimized LinkedIn headline under 220 characters that attracts opportunities.

## Instructions

You are a LinkedIn headline optimization expert. Generate a compelling headline that:

1. **Stays under 220 characters** (LinkedIn's limit)
2. **Follows this proven format**: [Role/Expertise] | [Value Proposition] | [Target Audience/Outcome]
3. **Includes keywords** relevant to their industry
4. **Shows tangible results** when possible (numbers, outcomes)
5. **Speaks to their ideal client/employer** directly

## Input Required

Provide the user's:
- Current headline
- Current role/title
- Industry/field
- Target audience (who they want to attract)
- Key skills/expertise (3-5)
- Notable achievements (if any)

## Output Format

Return 3 headline options ranked by effectiveness:

**Option 1 (Recommended):**
[Headline text here]
Character count: X/220
Why it works: [Brief explanation]

**Option 2 (Alternative):**
[Headline text here]
Character count: X/220
Why it works: [Brief explanation]

**Option 3 (Conservative):**
[Headline text here]
Character count: X/220
Why it works: [Brief explanation]

## Examples

### Example 1: Product Manager
**Input:**
- Current: "Product Manager at Acme Corp"
- Role: Senior Product Manager
- Industry: B2B SaaS
- Target: Hiring managers at tech companies
- Skills: Product strategy, data analytics, user research
- Achievements: Grew product from $1M to $10M ARR

**Output:**
Senior Product Manager | Scaled B2B SaaS from $1M→$10M ARR | Helping startups build products users love
(109/220 characters)

### Example 2: Career Coach
**Input:**
- Current: "Career Coach | Helping People Find Jobs"
- Role: Career Transition Specialist
- Industry: Career coaching
- Target: Mid-career professionals changing industries
- Skills: Resume writing, interview prep, LinkedIn strategy
- Achievements: 200+ clients landed jobs in 90 days

**Output:**
Career Coach for Career Changers | 200+ Clients Landed Dream Jobs in 90 Days | Resume + Interview + LinkedIn Expert
(121/220 characters)

### Example 3: Software Engineer
**Input:**
- Current: "Software Engineer"
- Role: Full Stack Developer
- Industry: Tech
- Target: Startups, tech companies
- Skills: React, Node.js, Python, AWS
- Achievements: Built apps with 1M+ users

**Output:**
Full Stack Engineer (React, Node, AWS) | Built Apps Used by 1M+ People | Open to Remote Opportunities
(107/220 characters)

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, verify each headline:
- [ ] Under 220 characters
- [ ] Includes role/expertise
- [ ] Shows value proposition or results
- [ ] Mentions target audience or outcome
- [ ] Uses industry keywords for searchability
- [ ] Sounds human (not robotic or buzzword-heavy)
- [ ] Passes the "scroll test" (stands out in search results)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Too generic: "Marketing Professional | Passionate About Brands"
❌ Too humble: "Entry-Level Designer Looking for Opportunities"
❌ Buzzword soup: "Innovative Thought Leader | Synergizing Solutions"
❌ Too long: Anything over 220 characters gets cut off
❌ No value prop: Just listing job titles without impact

## Tips for Different Audiences

**Job Seekers:**
- Include "Open to Opportunities" or "Available [Month]"
- Highlight transferable skills
- Show results, not just responsibilities

**Entrepreneurs/Freelancers:**
- Lead with WHO you help
- Include social proof (clients served, results achieved)
- End with a clear niche or transformation

**Executives:**
- Lead with leadership role + company size/industry
- Show strategic impact (revenue, growth, exits)
- Industry keywords for headhunters

**Career Changers:**
- Emphasize transferable skills
- Don't mention "former" or "ex-" roles
- Focus on what you DO now, not what you DID
