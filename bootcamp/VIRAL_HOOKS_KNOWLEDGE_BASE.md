## Hook Selection Strategy

### For Government Contractors Specifically:

**Best Performing Hooks** (based on engagement data):
1. Proof-driven posts with specific metrics ("I generated $X...")
2. Pattern/trend hooks ("I noticed a pattern after analyzing...")
3. Problem/solution hooks ("Stop doing X... Do Y instead")
4. Authority hooks ("I've helped X contractors...")

### Context Matching:

- **For Pain Point Content**: Use pattern/trend hooks or "What nobody tells you about..."
- **For Success Stories**: Use proof-driven hooks with metrics
- **For Educational Content**: Use list/framework hooks or "Top X" formats
- **For Thought Leadership**: Use controversial/bold hooks or "Unpopular opinion"

### Hook Structure Template:

```
[HOOK LINE] - Capture attention (first line)

[PROOF/CONTEXT] - 1-2 sentences establishing credibility or context

[VALUE] - 2-4 sentences providing actionable insights, patterns, or data

[CONNECTION] - 1-2 sentences connecting to audience's situation

[CALL TO ACTION] - Question, engagement prompt, or next step
```

---

## Integration Notes

- These hooks should be integrated into the content generation prompt chain
- Hooks can be combined with agency pain points for maximum relevance
- Different hooks work better for different post types (story-driven, stat-heavy, question-based)
- Always adapt hooks to sound natural and authentic, not formulaic
- Government contracting context requires more proof and authority than general B2B

## Usage Guidelines

1. **Don't Use Hooks Formulaically**: Adapt and make them authentic
2. **Match Hook to Content Type**: Proof hooks for success stories, pattern hooks for insights
3. **Combine with Pain Points**: Use agency-specific pain points within hook structures
4. **Include Proof**: Government contractors value credibility - add metrics, sources, data
5. **Maintain Authenticity**: The hook should sound like the user's voice, not a template
6. **Test and Iterate**: Track which hooks perform best for your audience
