# How to Use Government Contracting Data Sources

This guide explains how to leverage the authoritative sources in our knowledge base to find contract opportunities and understand agency priorities.

### 1. GSA Acquisition Gateway (acquisitiongateway.gov/forecast)

**What it is:**
- Centralized platform for federal agency procurement forecasts
- Replaced old hallways.cap.gsa.gov domain
- Early visibility into upcoming contract opportunities

**What you can find:**
- Expected federal contracting opportunities across agencies
- Small business set-aside designations
- Acquisition strategy status (planning vs. finalized)
- NAICS codes and anticipated award dates
- Contact information for acquisition officers

**How to use it:**
1. Visit [acquisitiongateway.gov/forecast](https://acquisitiongateway.gov/forecast)
2. Search by agency, NAICS code, or keywords
3. Filter for small business set-asides (8(a), WOSB, HUBZone, SDVOSB)
4. Look for "planning" status opportunities - **you can influence toward set-aside**
5. Note the contracting officer contact for questions

**Pro tip:** If an opportunity shows "planning" acquisition strategy, you can advocate for it to become a set-aside contract through small business outreach.

### 2. USAspending.gov API

**What it is:**
- Official US Treasury data on all federal spending
- Powers our Opportunity Scout contract search tool
- Real transaction data from FPDS (Federal Procurement Data System)

**What you can find:**
- Historical contract awards (last 3 fiscal years in our system)
- Actual spending by agency and office
- Set-aside contract data
- Contractor names and award amounts
- NAICS codes and locations

**How to use it:**
- Our Opportunity Scout tool queries this automatically
- Use it to identify agencies with strong set-aside spending in your industry
- Validate which offices actually award contracts vs. just planning

### 3. SAM.gov

**What it is:**
- System for Award Management - official government contracting portal
- Where active opportunities are posted
- Required registration for federal contractors

**What you can find:**
- Active solicitations and RFPs
- Contract opportunities by agency
- Past performance requirements
- Registration for your business

**How to use it:**
1. Register your business (required to bid)
2. Search by Agency ID codes from our Opportunity Scout results
3. Set up alerts for opportunities in your NAICS codes
4. Review past awards to understand competition

**Integration with our tool:**
- Agency ID links in Opportunity Scout link directly to SAM.gov searches
- Use our data to identify hot agencies, then search SAM.gov for their active opportunities

### 4. GAO High-Risk List & Reports

**What it is:**
- Government Accountability Office identifies critical agency challenges
- Biennial updates plus ongoing reports
- Official identification of pain points

**What you can find:**
- Agency-specific management challenges
- IT modernization needs
- Cybersecurity gaps
- Supply chain weaknesses
- Financial management issues

**How to use it:**
1. Visit [gao.gov/highrisk](https://www.gao.gov/highrisk)
2. Find agencies in your target market
3. Match GAO findings to your capabilities
4. Position your solutions as addressing documented pain points
5. Reference GAO reports in proposals to show awareness

**Example:** If GAO identifies "Legacy IT Modernization" as high-risk for an agency, and you provide IT modernization services, this is your opportunity to position expertise.

### 5. Agency Acquisition Forecasts (Individual Agency Sites)

**What it is:**
- Each agency's OSDBU (Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization) publishes forecasts
- Being migrated to GSA Acquisition Gateway
- Some agencies still maintain separate forecasts

**Key agency forecast sites:**
- **DoD:** [business.defense.gov](https://business.defense.gov)
- **DHS:** [dhs.gov/acquisition-forecast](https://www.dhs.gov/acquisition-forecast)
- **VA:** [va.gov/osdbu/acquisition-forecast](https://www.va.gov/osdbu/acquisition-forecast)
- **DOE:** [energy.gov/osbp/acquisition-forecast](https://www.energy.gov/osbp/acquisition-forecast)
- **HHS:** Agency-specific OSDBU pages

**How to use it:**
- Check both GSA Acquisition Gateway AND individual agency sites
- Look for multi-year IDIQ opportunities
- Note small business participation goals
- Identify upcoming recompetes of existing contracts

### 6. PSC Federal Business Forecast Scorecard

**What it is:**
- Professional Services Council annual evaluation of 70+ agency forecasts
- Grades agencies on forecast quality and transparency
- Published annually

**What you can find:**
- Which agencies have "Good" vs. "Needs Improvement" forecasts
- Quality of project descriptions
- Contact information availability
- Forecast accessibility issues

**How to use it:**
1. Visit [pscouncil.org/scorecard](https://www.pscouncil.org/scorecard)
2. Identify agencies with high-quality forecasts (easier to pursue)
3. Note agencies with poor forecasts (may need direct outreach)
4. Use as indicator of agency procurement maturity

**Pro tip:** Agencies with poor PSC scores may have opportunities with less competition due to information gaps.

### 7. SBA Procurement Scorecards

**What it is:**
- Small Business Administration grades agencies on small business goals
- Annual report cards (A+ to F)
- Tracks set-aside performance

**What you can find:**
- Agency performance on small business contracting goals
- Which agencies are meeting/missing 23% small business goal
- WOSB, 8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB goal performance
- Subcontracting data

**How to use it:**
1. Visit [sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-data/small-business-procurement-scorecard](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-data/small-business-procurement-scorecard)
2. Target agencies with HIGH grades (actively pursuing small business)
3. Also target agencies with LOW grades (under pressure to improve)
4. Use scorecard data to demonstrate market need in proposals

## ðŸŽ“ Advanced Tips

1. **Influence Set-Asides Early:** When GSA Acquisition Gateway shows "planning" status, reach out to the contracting officer about set-aside consideration.

2. **Cross-Reference Sources:** Verify forecast opportunities in GSA Gateway against actual spending history in our Opportunity Scout.

3. **Monitor GAO Reports:** Subscribe to GAO email alerts for your target agencies - pain points = opportunities.

4. **Track SBA Scorecard Trends:** Agencies with declining grades face pressure to increase small business awards.

5. **Use Agency ID Codes:** The codes in our Opportunity Scout link directly to SAM.gov searches - use them!

6. **Build Relationships:** Contact information in forecasts is gold - reach out for industry days and Q&A sessions.

7. **Timing Matters:** Engage during "planning" phase (forecasts) before requirements are finalized.
